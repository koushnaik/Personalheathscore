What is BMI?

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a widely used health metric that estimates body fat based on an individual's weight and height. It is commonly used by healthcare professionals as a screening tool to identify potential weight-related health risks.

BMI does not directly measure body fat, but it provides a reliable approximation for most individuals. Based on BMI, people are categorized as underweight, normal weight, overweight, or obese.

This calculator allows you to compute your BMI and automatically stores your results for future comparison. Over time, the system analyzes trends and provides personalized health suggestions.

BMI Categories

BMI Range Category Health Interpretation
Below 18.5 Underweight May indicate nutritional deficiency or other health issues
18.5 – 24.9 Normal Associated with lower risk of chronic diseases
25.0 – 29.9 Overweight May increase risk of cardiovascular problems
30.0 and above Obese High risk of serious health conditions

General BMI Improvement Tips

  • Maintain a balanced diet with adequate nutrients
  • Engage in regular physical activity
  • Monitor weight changes consistently
  • Avoid crash dieting or extreme eating habits
  • Consult healthcare professionals when necessary

The system provides additional personalized tips based on your BMI category and progress history.
